Introduction

Analyzing the release patterns and content characteristics of Netflix shows provides valuable insights into the evolution of streaming services and consumer preferences. For this study, we will utilize a dataset encompassing Netflix shows released between 2008 and 2021. This dataset not only highlights the company's strategic shifts but also sheds light on the variety of content offered to viewers over the years. It includes detailed information about each movie and series, such as the names of actors and directors, release dates, and categorical variables like content rating and type (TV Show or Movie). With over 7000 entries, the dataset reveals significant trends and gaps, such as missing director and cast member names, which have been addressed by imputing with "unknown" or removing overly detailed descriptions. By visualizing this data, we aim to uncover patterns in release volumes and other key metrics, enhancing our understanding of Netflix's content strategy and its impact on the video-on-demand landscape.

Selection of Data Mining Algorithm and Data Pre-processing:

For the chosen data mining algorithm that will be implemented is the time series algorithm. Time series is collecting numerical data in sequential time intervals. The time intervals can be (yearly, quarterly, monthly, weekly and more depending on the data used). A time-series plot shows the variable of interest against the interval of time. In our case our dataset for the release date of titles we have information up to the date where an instance of a title was added, our variable of interest, in this case, will be the number of titles released and the interval of time will be the release dates of titles

In this graph that was used for the data visualisation, we can observe that there is a trend in the popularity of stand-up comedy shows, based on this data we can start making predictions on future trends, this can also be applied for different genres.The main variables that will be used for the data mining section will be the count of titles released and the release dates. The first major alteration to the dataset was that the release date section was formatted to DD/MM/YY, there were some instances where data in regards to the release date was missing, so it was decided to delete those instances of data as we cannot use them, further, there were instances where the date was not showing in the right format, that data was also removed from the dataset. Each table row was transformed to the right format type e.g.(in the type row in our data it is set to be either TV-Show or Movie).

Data Ethics:

Regarding data ethics relating to data analysis, the main concern here is Privacy, it has to be considered that one has the right to:Confidentiality - meaning that one can limit the spread of information about themselves.Anonymity- stating that anyone has the right to be free from unwanted attentionSolitude- avoidance of physical proximity to others.The concerns regarding the ethical issue are the private information of individuals, and how it can affect them, in general, data mining is capable of identifying individuals from a large amount of anonymous data. In order to protect private data there have to be security measures implemented, but the responsibility falls under the organisation, government, and businesses in other words the responsibility falls under the organisation that is collecting that information. In order to regulate data collection in order to prevent security violations the GDPR was invented, its main purpose is to clear the concerns of customers, regulators and other groups regarding the safety of personnel data. Another purpose of the GDPR is to account for the swift development of modern data processing techniques and technologies.Looking at the professional issue is that computer professionals must design their systems in a way that the user is aware of what information is being stored about them and another requirement for a system is that it should allow an individual to customise the levels of privacy like the level of security and the level of acceptable recording/monitoring/tracking. Professionals have a duty to implement adequate security measures, meaning the data has to be preserved in a way that third parties will not interfere, an example of this will be preserving the data through either encryption or anonymisation.
